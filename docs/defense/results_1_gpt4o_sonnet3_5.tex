\section{Data Source}

This report uses the frozen pre-June-11 Spanish-time snapshot in
\path{results/api_models_pre_2026_06_11_es}. The snapshot was extracted
from commit \code{96e05c3}
(\emph{Llava finetuning fix}), dated 2026-06-10 22:37:44 +0200.

The available Claude file in that snapshot is named
\code{claude-sonnet-4-5}. The comparison below uses that stored result set as
the Sonnet side of the requested GPT-4o versus Sonnet analysis.

\section{Focus on Type A and Type B Benchmarks}

Type A contains visual question answering and visual reasoning benchmarks.
Type B contains detection and localization benchmarks. The June 10 snapshot has
six shared Type A benchmarks and six shared Type B benchmarks for this pair.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\caption{Type A/B matched summary across 12 shared benchmarks.}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}lrrrr@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Model} & \textbf{Benchmarks} & \textbf{Mean score} & \textbf{Median score} & \textbf{Mean normalized} \\
\midrule
\code{gpt-4o} & 12 & 0.450 & 0.458 & 0.833 \\
\code{claude-sonnet-4-5} & 12 & 0.322 & 0.293 & 0.250 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\caption{Type A/B family means.}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{@{}llrrr@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Family} & \textbf{Model} & \textbf{Benchmarks} & \textbf{Mean score} & \textbf{Mean rank} \\
\midrule
Type A / QA & \code{gpt-4o} & 6 & 0.800 & 1.00 \\
Type A / QA & \code{claude-sonnet-4-5} & 6 & 0.508 & 2.00 \\
Type B / Detection & \code{gpt-4o} & 6 & 0.100 & 1.33 \\
Type B / Detection & \code{claude-sonnet-4-5} & 6 & 0.135 & 1.50 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

GPT-4o is clearly ahead on Type A QA benchmarks. Type B is more mixed:
Claude has a higher raw detection mean, while GPT-4o has the better average
rank and normalized score because the wins are distributed differently across
the six detection benchmarks.
